# Privacy Policy

**Last Updated: March 25, 2026**

## 1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy describes how Lux Industries Inc. and its affiliates ("Lux Industries," "we," "us," or "our") collect, use, and share information when you access or use the Lux Network protocol interfaces (the "Interface"), including the Lux Exchange, Lux Bridge, Lux Explorer, and associated websites and applications.

**THE LUX NETWORK PROTOCOL IS DECENTRALIZED AND NON-CUSTODIAL. WE DO NOT COLLECT, STORE, OR HAVE ACCESS TO YOUR PRIVATE KEYS, SEED PHRASES, WALLET PASSWORDS, OR THE CONTENTS OF YOUR WALLET.**

## 2. Information We Do NOT Collect

Because the Interface connects to a decentralized, non-custodial protocol:

- **We do NOT collect your private keys, seed phrases, or wallet passwords.** Your wallet is controlled exclusively by you. We never have access to your signing credentials.
- **We do NOT create user accounts.** The Interface does not require registration. Your blockchain address serves as your pseudonymous identity.
- **We do NOT store your funds or assets.** All digital assets remain in your self-custody wallet or in decentralized smart contracts on the blockchain. We have no ability to access, freeze, seize, or transfer your assets.
- **We do NOT control your transactions.** All transactions are executed directly by you on the blockchain. We cannot reverse, modify, or cancel transactions.
- **We do NOT collect financial information.** We do not collect bank account numbers, credit card numbers, Social Security numbers, or other traditional financial identifiers through the base Interface.

## 3. Information We May Collect

### 3.1 Information You Provide

- **Feedback and support.** If you contact us for support, we may collect your email address and the content of your communication.
- **Voluntary surveys or forms.** If you choose to participate in surveys or fill out forms, we collect the information you provide.

### 3.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you access the Interface, we may automatically collect:

- **Public blockchain data.** Your public blockchain address and transaction history are visible on the public blockchain. This data is not "collected" by us — it exists on a public, decentralized ledger accessible to anyone.
- **Device and usage information.** We may collect browser type, operating system, device type, screen resolution, language preference, referring URL, and pages visited within the Interface. This data is collected via privacy-respecting analytics (Hanzo Insights) and does not identify you personally.
- **IP address.** Your IP address may be temporarily processed for security purposes (DDoS protection, rate limiting) via our infrastructure provider. IP addresses are not stored long-term or linked to wallet addresses.
- **Cookies and local storage.** The Interface may use cookies and browser local storage for user preferences (theme, language, recent tokens, slippage settings). These are functional cookies that improve your experience. No tracking cookies are used.

### 3.3 Wallet Address Screening

The Interface uses third-party compliance screening services (including TRM Labs) to check wallet addresses against global sanctions lists (OFAC SDN, EU/UK sanctions). This screening:

- Is performed when you connect your wallet to the Interface
- Sends your public wallet address (which is already public on the blockchain) to the screening provider
- Does NOT send any personal information, private keys, or transaction details
- May result in restricted access to the Interface if your address is flagged
- Is subject to the screening provider's own privacy policy

**Important:** This screening applies only to the Interface. The underlying Protocol is permissionless and cannot be restricted. You can always interact with the Protocol directly via smart contract calls, command-line tools, or alternative interfaces.

## 4. How We Use Information

We use collected information for:

- **Operating the Interface.** Serving web pages, maintaining functionality, and fixing bugs.
- **Security.** Protecting against DDoS attacks, spam, and abuse. Screening wallet addresses against sanctions lists.
- **Analytics.** Understanding aggregate usage patterns to improve the Interface. We use privacy-respecting analytics that do not track individual users across the web.
- **Legal compliance.** Complying with applicable laws, regulations, and legal processes.
- **Communication.** Responding to support inquiries you initiate.

We do NOT use your information for:

- Targeted advertising or behavioral profiling
- Selling to third parties for marketing purposes
- Building user profiles linked to real-world identities
- Surveillance or monitoring of individual user behavior

## 5. How We Share Information

We do NOT sell your personal information to anyone.

We may share information in limited circumstances:

- **Service providers.** With infrastructure providers (hosting, CDN, analytics) who process data on our behalf under contractual obligations to protect your privacy.
- **Compliance screening.** With sanctions screening providers (e.g., TRM Labs) as described in Section 3.3. Only your public wallet address is shared.
- **Legal requirements.** When required by law, subpoena, court order, or governmental request. We will notify you if legally permitted to do so.
- **Safety.** To protect the rights, property, or safety of Lux Industries, our users, or the public.
- **Business transfers.** In connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, with notice to users.

## 6. On-Chain Data

All transactions on the Lux Network (and any public blockchain) are **publicly visible and permanently recorded** on the blockchain. This includes:

- Transaction amounts and token types
- Sender and recipient addresses
- Transaction timestamps
- Smart contract interactions
- Staking deposits and withdrawals
- Liquidity provision and removal
- Bridge operations
- Governance votes

**This on-chain data is inherently public.** It is not "collected" by Lux Industries — it exists on a decentralized, public ledger. Lux Industries does not have the ability to delete, modify, or restrict access to on-chain data. You should assume that any transaction you execute on a public blockchain is permanently visible to anyone.

## 7. Data Retention

- **Analytics data** is retained in aggregate form and automatically purged after 90 days.
- **IP addresses** are not stored long-term. They may be temporarily processed by infrastructure providers for security purposes.
- **Support communications** are retained for as long as necessary to resolve your inquiry, plus a reasonable period for legal compliance.
- **On-chain data** is permanent and cannot be deleted by any party.

## 8. Your Rights and Choices

### 8.1 Analytics Opt-Out

You can opt out of analytics collection through the Privacy Choices menu in the Interface. This disables non-essential analytics while preserving core functionality.

### 8.2 Cookie Management

You can manage cookies through your browser settings. Blocking all cookies may affect Interface functionality (e.g., saved preferences).

### 8.3 Wallet Disconnection

You can disconnect your wallet from the Interface at any time. This immediately stops any interaction between your wallet and the Interface.

### 8.4 Data Access and Deletion

Because we do not create user accounts or store personal profiles, there is generally no personal data to access, correct, or delete. If you have contacted us for support and wish to have your communication deleted, contact us at privacy@lux.network.

### 8.5 Do Not Track

The Interface respects "Do Not Track" browser signals. When DNT is enabled, we disable non-essential analytics.

## 9. Security

We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect the limited information we process. However:

- No system is 100% secure
- The primary security of your assets depends on your private key management
- We cannot guarantee the security of information transmitted over the internet
- Blockchain transactions are irreversible — there is no "undo" for on-chain actions

## 10. Children's Privacy

The Interface is not directed to persons under 18 years of age. We do not knowingly collect information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will delete it.

## 11. International Users

The Interface is available globally. Information may be processed in the United States and other countries where our service providers operate. By using the Interface, you consent to the transfer of information to countries outside your country of residence, which may have different data protection laws.

### 11.1 European Economic Area (EEA) and UK

If you are in the EEA or UK, you have additional rights under GDPR/UK GDPR, including the right to access, rectification, erasure, restriction of processing, data portability, and objection. Given the minimal data we collect and our non-custodial architecture, most of these rights are satisfied by default. Contact privacy@lux.network for any GDPR requests.

### 11.2 California Residents

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) provides additional rights. We do not sell personal information. We do not use personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising.

## 12. Third-Party Links and Protocols

The Interface may contain links to third-party websites, protocols, or services. This Privacy Policy does not apply to third-party services. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party services you interact with.

When you interact with third-party protocols through the Interface (e.g., bridging to other blockchains, interacting with DeFi protocols), those interactions are governed by the respective protocol's terms and your own due diligence.

## 13. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on the Interface with an updated "Last Updated" date. Your continued use of the Interface after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
